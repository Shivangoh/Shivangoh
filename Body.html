  • Math Activity

Duration:20mins

Tools used: Excursions


  1. Write numbers in ascending order on one side of the sticky notes. On the other side of every sticky note, draw an object that the students will have to find. The cards that state numbers less than five should have an object that's hard to find.

  2. The number of sticky notes should be the same as the number of students in the classroom

  3. Place the pile of sticky notes on a table, and ask the students to come up one by one to take one sticky note each.

  4. Based on the numbered side of the sticky note, students have to find the object drawn on the other side that many times.

  5. Based on the numbered side of the sticky note, students have to find the object drawn on the other side that many times.

  • English Activity

Duration:30mins

Tools used: Forced Connections


  1. Divide the class into two teams with one teacher maintaining each group.

  2. Tell each team to come up with a story through the use of the tool 'Forced Connections'.

  3. Give them an unfamiliar object to create a story related to friendship.

  4. Give them crayons and paper to draw or write down their ideas.

  5. Facilitate them by giving them encouragement, give them prompts about what they need to do, and how much time they have to do it.

  • Social Studies Activity

Duration:30mins

Tools used: Hits


  1. First, put up a cut-out of a house on the wall. On top, create a legend which shows different colors representing different members found within a family.

  2. Now, ask the students to create a simple drawing of a house.

  3. Once they're done, distribute sticky notes that are the same colors at the colors in the legend on the wall.

  4. Ask the students to stick colored sticky notes on the drawing of their house to represent what members make up their family.

  5. In the end, help all the students put up their house drawings around the cut-out on the wall.

  6. Explain to the students the relationships between family members in a family, and how every family is unique. Point out the uniqueness based on the students' drawings of their houses and families.
